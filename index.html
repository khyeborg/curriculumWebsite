Introduction to Exponential Functions (b value)

Student Learning Objective

SWBAT investigate the effects changing the base number in an exponential function can have on its curve IOT begin their study of exponential functions.

Lesson and Outcome

The equation/formula for an exponential function, f(x) = a(b)x, is introduced in this lesson. Students are given an “a” value and several b values and are asked to substitute these values into the calculator to study the characteristics of each curve. By the end of the lesson students should be able to identify the type of curve that should be expected given a b value greater than 1 or a b value less than 1 but greater than 0.

Conceptual Extension

Why can’t b = 1? How does this tie into what we know about exponents?

Introduction to Exponential Functions (a and b values)

Student Learning Objective

SWBAT investigate the effects changing the base number and starting value in an exponential function can have on its curve IOT continue their study of exponential functions.

Lesson and Outcome

The students continue their study of exponential functions f(x) = a(b)x, within this lesson. Students are given several “a” and “b” values and are asked to substitute these values into the calculator to study the characteristics of each curve. By the end of the lesson students should be able to identify the type of curve that should be expected given an “a” value that is smaller/bigger or negative/positive.

Conceptual Extension

In what situation would our starting value be a negative number? In what situation would our starting value be zero?

Appreciation and Depreciation using Equations

Student Learning Objective

SWBAT connect exponential functions to real world scenarios IOT utilize them to model and predict outcomes. Part 1.

Lesson and Outcome

The students continue their study of exponential functions f(x) = a(b)x, within this lesson. Students are given word problems related to appreciating and depreciating values and use the information from these problems to create an equation that models the scenario. Once the students create the equation, they will be asked to create a follow up question that can be answered using the equation they have created and respond to it.

Conceptual Extension

What other type of real world problems utilize the notation of appreciating and depreciating values?

Appreciation and Depreciation using Graphs/Visuals

Student Learning Objective

SWBAT connect exponential functions to real world scenarios IOT utilize them to model and predict outcomes. Part 2.

Lesson and Outcome

The students continue their study of exponential functions f(x) = a(b)x, within this lesson. Students are expected to take the information from the previous day's lesson and use technology to develop a table of values and graph the curve matching their information. The students should use the graph they have created to visual show their prediction.

Conceptual Extension

What are the benefits of using a graph to draw conclusions and make predictions instead of an equation? What are the benefits of using an equation to draw conclusions and make predictions instead of a graph?

Gathering Information for Community Analysis

Student Learning Objective

SWBAT collect and organize the data found in the “COVID Alert NY” application IOT begin studying the spread of COVID within their neighborhoods. Part 1.

Lesson and Outcome

The students continue their study of exponential functions f(x) = a(b)x, within this lesson. Students are expected to collect the information from the “COVID Alert NY” application. Students should be looking at the section of the city that represents their borough. They should be collecting information such as a percentage from that given day and number of people infected at the moment. Using this information the students will create an exponential equation, create a trend, and study this trend using an equation, table, and graph. Use of calculators will be allowed to support findings.

Conceptual Extension

Based on the data in the application, are there any flaws we need to account for?

Gathering Information for Community Analysis 2

Student Learning Objective

Does any of your information from the previous day need to be changed based on what the application is reporting today?

Drawing Conclusions Between Different Counties

Student Learning Objective

SWBAT compare and contrast the trends studied over the last 2 lessons IOT continue studying the spread of COVID within their neighborhoods.

Lesson and Outcome

The students continue their study of exponential functions f(x) = a(b)x, within this lesson. Students have created 2 equations and 2 trends over the last 2 lessons. They are going to take this time to compare and contrast the information they have gathered in order to draw a conclusion about the spread in other places compared to their own neighborhood. Some students may conclude that the spread in the neighborhood of choice is similar to the spread in their own neighborhood. Some might choose to want to move to the neighborhood they choose to study. And some students will realize that their neighborhood might just be in better standings than most.

Conceptual Extension

The students can look up the population breakdown in these places to see if there are any clusters of ethnic or religious New Yorkers that could also be used within the conversation of COVID 19 spread.

How does COVID 19 being close to home make you feel?

Student Learning Objective

SWBAT reflect on the trends studied about their own neighborhoods IOT reflect on their feelings about COVID 19 being so close to home.

Lesson and Outcome

The students wrap up their study of the COVID 19 spread with their neighborhood but reflecting on a list of questions. They will first have time to reflect individually and think about what has changed in their lives and how they feel about the spread. They will then create 3 questions of their own to be able to interview a peer. As a pair/group the students will then come up with next steps or predictions about what they believe will happen in NY in the next couple of weeks? Months? Season?

Conceptual Extension

How do you feel Governor Cuomo could help you and your family feel better about living in a neighborhood with a high spread rate of COVID 19?

Overview

Introduction

Welcome to the Computer Science section of the “Understanding the Pandemic” curriculum! In the Computer Science section, students will engage in hands-on coding projects using the p5 Coding Library to build simulations to visualize the spread of COVID-19. The Computer Science section of the curriculum aims to instill awareness about the mechanics of the spread of COVID-19 using culturally sustaining pedagogy and authentic assessments. Through the simulations and coding projects, students will better understand the underlying mechanics of COVID-19 from an analytical perspective to better protect themselves, each other, and their families.

Culturally Sustaining Pedagogy

In the Day 7: Virus Variables Input section, students will make use of the COVID Alert NY app and other reliable COVID-19 data sources to gain accurate data to run disease spread simulations that reflect the difference in spread in neighborhoods of different demographics, especially contrasting the difference in spread, recovery, and mortality rates between predominantly white neighborhoods and minority neighborhoods. Students are asked to collect data of the demographics distribution in different cities, select a predominantly white neighborhood and a minority neighborhood, and collect COVID-19 data in the cities selected. Students will use the data collected to run two simulations and record their observations to be used in further discussions centered on reasons for the difference in spread and mortality.

Authentic Assessment

In the assessment section, students will use the existing code as the foundation and implement additional features to enhance the COVID-19 simulation to better reflect the real time spread of COVID-19. Students are introduced to the assessment and guided to formulate additional features to be added to the existing simulation to better reflect the real time spread of COVID-19. Students are encouraged to tackle the assessment from any aspects of the virus, based on their field of interests. Two sample projects with downloadable source code can be found to introduce students to some possible ways of enhancing the simulation.
